10.13.23 - history of neocities buttons [to contents] Ever wondered why Neocities buttons are such a weird resolution? Well, I can save you a minute on Google. Netscape is credited with the first usage of the 88x31 format, appearing on their web browser's navigation elements. Its spread to personal websites however, was due to their Netscape Now! program where sites that fulfilled certain technical requirements could display a little button effectively saying they were up to date on trends. GeoCities was a hosting service started in the late 90s, which is also where Neocities gets its name from (GeoCities was styled with a capital "c" so shouldn't "Neocities" be too?). Anyway, whether they were directly referencing the Netscape Now! badge format or if other "badges" had already begun to appear at that resolution, part of the deal for free hosting was that you had to link back to the main site via an 88x31 gif. The gif clashed with many users' themes so they often customized it, leading to user-made buttons with all sorts of messages became a staple of personal sites. sources: [1], [2], [3]


10.13.23 - LCD [to contents] I think "corporations" are blamed by default for everything when in reality it's more often than not consumers' apathy towards critical thinking and making informed decisions that these entities merely capitalize (heh) off of. A symptom rather than the root. When a video game company ships something buggy and incomplete, each person that buys it anyway enables that behavior. Or what about the infamous "thumbnail face" youtubers do? They don't do it because they want to, (well, at least the ones with a shred of pride don't) but because it makes ctr go insane at virtually no cost (aforementioned loss of pride excluded). And what's ctr? How often someone, seeing a thumbnail, decides to click on that video and watch it. Society is dragged down by the Lowest Common Denominator. If we're going to complain about someone, forget the evil corporations or obnoxious youtubers, direct it at the source. People too stupid or apathetic to consider the ethical weight of their consumption habits, easily bored, easily amused, and engaged by obscenities like oversaturated colors and a goofy face on a thumbnail. Yes, some consumers economically do not have a choice. Ethically sourced materials for any product nearly by premise are going to be more expensive. But someone buying a 70USD video game clearly has disposable income. Higher standards are attained through an increase in the acceptance of shaming culture. Obviously that comes with its own problems but there's room in between extremes.


10.08.23 - which side? [to contents] Some statistics about the relative frequency of which 'side' of the body is favored: right hand/left hand: 90/10 right eye/left eye: 70/30 regular/goofy (which foot leads for things like skateboarding and surfing): 56/44 regular is left foot forward, goofy is right Find out which eye is dominant for you: Make a frame with your hands like this and close one eye at a time. On one side the position won't change and on the other it will shift a bit. The eye that doesn't change your view is your dominant one. Find out which foot is dominant for you: Leap forward and land as though you were jumping onto an icy surface. The foot that leads is your dominant one for stability. Alternatively if you have friends have one shove you from behind, and see which foot leads to catch yourself. The traits aren't too strongly associated. Although there is apparently a correlation between eye and hand dominance, exceptions are quite common. I am right-handed, left-eye dominant, and have a regular stance. Ear dominance seemed to have some conflicting views on its existence or not. Whatever the case, clearly it's far less pronounced compared to the other types. Ambidexterity is somewhat misunderstood, although children sometimes don't have a strong preference for one hand or the other, muscle memory must be independently developed on each side regardless. Someone won't automatically be equally proficient at tasks with either arm, in other words. It's just a matter of regular usage on that side. Because I like to learn useless skills I've been practicing writing with my left hand again. I've gotten further this time around.

09.25.23 - the nail that sticks up gets hammered down [to contents] The title is a Japanese proverb which seems to embody the general ethos of east asia concerning how they present themselves. Humility (or at least a show of it), staying in your own lane, not causing trouble for others, communal duty/expectations seem to be much more prevalent in their culture. It's interesting to see how that translates online, where content produced by asians tends to be a lot more focused on the artistic work or topic with minimal "personality" coming through. This is noticeable in how people present themselves online. A lot of asian accounts often will be a incomprehensible string of letters and numbers, and while of course this has developed into a stylistic thing as much as anything else, the root is in the de-emphasis on distinguishing the self. Or in the league youtube space, there's lots of people where their handle is just [champion name]king, which is another implicit indentification with the larger group. This is in comparison to the American or at least western ideal where the "individual" is king. And based on the atmosphere around college-age youth, it's only getting stronger. Obviously both have downsides, diminishment of self perpetuates more of an atmosphere of "shame" where personal problems must stay shoved down for fear of being unsightly and a burden which.. probably isn't healthy. And the quality of communal structures and spaces suffers if everyone only pursues their own self-interests. I'm pretty sure the average park or movie theater has a good deal more litter in the US than most of east asia.


09.23.23 - simpler, stupid [to contents] Trying to apply the KISS rule more. This current website version is a lot more in line with that idea now. It used to be more elaborate but it wasn't really worth the extra time. The website should mainly be a means of presenting content anyhow, and less of a point of interest in itself. Anyhow, this applies to all other creative activities. In painting I was always trying to showcase technical skill everywhere with communicating any particular idea a distant thought but that's kind of a tiring and unending pursuit.. I think it makes a lot more sense to aggressively pare down the scope of any creative thing and just focus on making that as good as possible. An obvious sentiment, but all the more important to be reminded of.


08.20.23 - reason and emotion [to contents] Better a miserable scholar or satisfied pig ? Well, only one of them is happy.. Over the years, there has certainly been no shortage of wrestling with the 'human condition'; attempting to resolve an 'unreasonable' universe. There's an enormous wealth of paper on whether we can truly know anything, whether the senses are reliable.. etc. But it's never sat well with me... and isn't that what's important? Emotion is the only experience; disconcertment over one existential quandry or another is just that, a negative emotion, no matter its origin. At the end of it, reasoning is far less immediate than emotion, a quality that we experience self-evidently. Reasoning can only incite emotion, not the other way around.
