intelligence types

sept 23 23

The evidence for this post is that I made it up. They're called 'intelligences' to imply that they are more or less set qualities after the brain fully develops, whatever that means. The 'major types' are named that because they can be used for a fairly complete general read of someone's cognitive facility. Additionally they are very broad traits that come up in almost every activity. As for the minor types, I've again tried to make types where each is independent of each other. They don't always or even usually derive from the major types. major types:

  1. computational, deductive
    • the best known mental capacity, often mistaken for cognitive ability as a whole
    • this intelligence most directly benefits from a large working memory
    • applying rulesets to find answers systematically
    • extremely overrepresented in schoolwork
  2. person-centric
    • how well you understand humans; yourself and other people
    • crucial for being able to create a desired effect through art, or rhetorical speech
  3. pattern recognition
    • somewhat encapsulated by common sense, but it's certainly not equally present in people
    • 'soft skill' - basically the antithesis to the deductive trait
    • the quality of philosophical reasoning, just a gradation
minor types:
  1. linguistic-articulate
    • seems to be more or less one-to-one to general mental acuity, that is, best served by strength along all the major types.
  2. simulative
    • strong spatial awareness, proprioception, sense of balance
    • an intuition for qualities like inertia and mass
    • very useful for that egg drop school project..
  3. physical dexterity
    • how precise your fine motor skills are
    • handwriting, typing, drawing
    • maybe a subset of physic-simulative
    • hazily tied to general mental acuity..
  4. creativity
    • more like a sub-subtype since this quality is highly abstract and more than a little subjective
    • a coefficient of how observant you are and how much person-centric intelligence you have
  5. observant...ness
    • how much you take notice of sensory qualities in the environment
    • more or less self-explanatory. not sure if this can really be tied into stuff any better..
    • possibly related to autism or adjacent
