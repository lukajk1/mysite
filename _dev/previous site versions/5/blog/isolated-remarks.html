The points we individually differ on the most from everyone else are the most important things to write about, I think. And in that vein they are maybe by definition the most interesting things to read about from other people. People working on their linux setups remind me of people working on their cars. Not that I have an issue with either.. Something is not worth reading/watching/listening to just because it 'created' a genre, or was the first to suggest/popularize an idea. A relatively larger amount of 'originality' is not really valuable on its own. It's best to give problems in philosophy some amount of work yourself prior to reading other people's thoughts. It makes it much more like a conversation where there's a back and forth.. And it makes their argument more memorable.
