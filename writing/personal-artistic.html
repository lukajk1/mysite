Formalism is an idea in media criticism that means to only or at least emphasize the instrinsic qualities of a work with contextual details such as the circumstances around the work's creation as of distant secondary importance. I was reminded of the idea of "music for musicians" recently, and I realized it more or less encapsulated my main interest. It's less of an established idea in other arts but that's how I want to do things. Not everything I do has to fall in line with this either though, of course. As with everything the specifics of a formalist breakdown varies between critics but I will extend the definition to include a general de-emphasis on subject matter. While the associations that the subject matter invoke are still important to me they are fairly less so compared to others' preferences. This includes subject matter in artwork and lyrics in music as well as somewhat less explicit signifiers such as a work being dedicated to a person or institution of some sort--generally that song becomes strongly colored by the perception of that entity but I prefer to separate them, at least somewhat. Aside from that I think the sensory component that art engages is pleasing for its own sake. Colors and shapes or patterns of notes that work well together are the basic examples of this. As far as my current interests for game design go they focus on trying to figure out the consequences of mechanics with the same de-emphasis on the "fiction" or immersive elements. In the past I had a much stronger emphasis on good 'craftsmanship' or technique virtually to the exclusion of all else. Recently though, I've lent subject matter more importance. However, the formalist approach is still very much at the center of my interest.
