09.27.23 - tropes? [back to contents] There's nothing inherently wrong with being media being tropey. The difference between good and bad writing is the characters. If they're wooden and lacking appeal, relying on tropes to make stuff happen, then that's poor characterization. Reliance on tropes is only a symptom. The importance of originality is overstated, if such a thing existed anyhow


09.25.23 - the nail that sticks up gets hammered down [back to contents] The title is a Japanese proverb which seems to embody the general ethos of east asia concerning how they present themselves. Humility (or at least a show of it), staying in your own lane, not causing trouble for others, communal duty/expectations seem to be much more prevalent in their culture. It's interesting to see how that translates online, where content produced by asians tends to be a lot more focused on the artistic work or topic with minimal "personality" coming through. This is noticeable in how people present themselves online. A lot of asian accounts often will be a incomprehensible string of letters and numbers, and while of course this has developed into a stylistic thing as much as anything else, the root is in the de-emphasis on distinguishing the self. Or in the league youtube space, there's lots of people where their handle is just [champion name]king, which is another implicit indentification with the larger group. This is in comparison to the American or at least western ideal where the "individual" is king. And based on the atmosphere around college-age youth, it's only getting stronger. Obviously both have downsides, diminishment of self perpetuates more of an atmosphere of "shame" where personal problems must stay shoved down for fear of being unsightly and a burden which.. probably isn't healthy. And the quality of communal structures and spaces suffers if everyone only pursues their own self-interests. I'm pretty sure the average park or movie theater has a good deal more litter in the US than most of east asia.


09.23.23 - simpler, stupid [back to contents] Trying to apply the KISS rule more. This current website version is a lot more in line with that idea now. It used to be more elaborate but it wasn't really worth the extra time. The website should mainly be a means of presenting content anyhow, and less of a point of interest in itself. Anyhow, this applies to all other creative activities. In painting I was always trying to showcase technical skill everywhere with communicating any particular idea a distant thought but that's kind of a tiring and unending pursuit.. I think it makes a lot more sense to aggressively pare down the scope of any creative thing and just focus on making that as good as possible. An obvious sentiment, but all the more important to be reminded of.
