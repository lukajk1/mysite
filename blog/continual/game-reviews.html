08.29.23 | Sekiro - 8/10 It's difficult to find anything in singleplayer games similar to the satisfaction of the deflection mechanic and beating the bosses in this game. For the most part, it's you getting better at the game that's driving it forward. There's also Fromsoft's usual breadth of enemy designs and environments, a particularly memorable area being Foutainhead Palace. It also has a fair bit of replayability with the gauntlets, increasing difficulty of ng+, and an alternate story branch.


08.29.23 | Hogwarts Legacy - 6/10 Without the massive brand name behind this game, it would be a completely unremarkable rpg. It doesn't do anything terrible, it's just kind of bland. I found the story pretty unappealing, I wasn't interested much in saving the world like you have to do in half of rpgs. Although, maybe it can be said it was more a fault with the execution rather than the outline. I disliked doing the main dungeons in the storyline because of their tedious nature. The puzzles weren't great but were much less of a problem than the combat. Given how prominently it features in the game and thematically (like 80% of the appeal of Harry Potter is doing magic stuff), it was boring. There was little enemy variation, and pretty much every scenario could be handled the same way. They did opt somewhat for the standard "this thing is more effective against this thing" but color matching really isn't any more interesting on its own. I maybe wish that they had focused on fewer spells and really making those interactions meaningful but I can understand it would have soured a selling point. Another large problem is that there really isn't much to do if you don't like hunting down every little collectible. There's also little that ties everything together; what you do at and around Hogwarts isn't directly related to the main story at all. I've seen some comments that the game could benefit from a morality meter, and I agree with the general idea - the game just doesn't make you feel like your choices or actions have impact on your environment. I'm constantly blasting unforgivables and classmates won't bat an eye.. Visually, this game does represent the best of the western "realism/fidelity" approach so it does get points there. Lighting, particularly in caves, also left a strong impression at times.


06.04.23 | Mirror's Edge Catalyst - 5/10 Catalyst is a pretty middling experience. Its distinct art direction is the only noteworthy factor; practically every other aspect struggles to meet average. The game's development just feels severely rushed. The game is open world, but the main quest levels are tightly railroaded, offering little in terms of alternative solutions to the platforming. Similarly, side quests feel like they were built out in a few days, there's little variation and recycled assets. The combat fails in the same vein. It's sufficient to spam a handful of the options and the "wanted" system doesn't escalate in an interesting way, capping out quickly once again having minimal variety in enemies and interactions. Going back to the open world environment, we see the same issue. There's virtually nothing to see other than the same reductive cubist white-on-black architecture everywhere, no NPCs to talk to outside of the cookie cutter side quests/challenges mentioned, and hardly people in sight at all, come to mention. It can't be argued that the homogeneity of the architecture is some sort of statement about monopolistic corpo-governance or anything like that, it's clearly a time saving technique. There's plently more examples of its shortcomings, but there's been too much repetitiveness already. In short, the game feels unfinished, and the main quest only gets a recommendation if you happen to be into parkour and its particular art style.
