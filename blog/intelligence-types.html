1- computational, deductive - the best known mental capacity, often mistaken for it itself. - this intelligence most directly benefits from a large working memory - rigorous application of rules to find answers systematically 2 - person-centric - understanding other people's feelings and being able to anticipate how they will react to things crucial for being able to create a desired effect through art, or rhetorical speech - can be used to understand yourself as well - most people have a far worse understanding than they think - knowing the "right things to say" in varied social situations 3 - abstractive, analogizing - concieve of phenomena in more general terms, forming relationships that would otherwise go overlooked minor types: - linguistic - this is almost a subset of person-centric intelligence. speech is highly contextual, so having a good read on a person's intent goes a long way towards - physic-simulative - strong spatial awareness, proprioception, sense of balance - an intuition for qualities like inertia and mass - very useful for that egg drop school project.. - dance, skateboarding, martial arts - dexterity - not sure exactly how this trait fits in but it's clear that it's a quality people independently posses in varying amounts. - hazily tied to general mental acuity.. - artistic-creative - basically a coefficient of how observant you are and how much person-centric intelligence you have
