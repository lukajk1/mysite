briefly, reasoning and emotion

aug 20 23

Better a miserable scholar or satisfied pig ? Well, only one of them is happy.. Over the years, there has certainly been no shortage of wrestling with the 'human condition'; attempting to resolve an 'unreasonable' universe. There's an enormous wealth of paper on whether we can truly know anything, whether the senses are reliable.. etc. But it's never sat well with me... and isn't that what's important? Emotion is the only experience; disconcertment over one existential quandry or another is just that, a negative emotion, no matter its origin. At the end of it, reasoning is far less immediate than emotion, a quality that we experience self-evidently. Reasoning can only incite emotion, not the other way around.
